SUPPORT & RESISTANCE
Visual Guide

Support & Resistance

The foundation of every chart analysis. Once you see these, you cannot unsee them.

Lesson 1 of 5
What are they?

Support is a price level where buying pressure has historically been strong enough to stop a decline. Think of it as a floor — price tends to bounce up from it.

Resistance is a price level where selling pressure has historically been strong enough to stop a rally. Think of it as a ceiling — price tends to reverse down from it.

Lesson 2 of 5
Why do they exist?

These levels exist because of market memory. When many traders bought at $45,000 and price dropped, they remember that price. When price returns to $45,000, many of them sell to break even — creating resistance.

The same logic works for support: buyers who missed a previous rally wait at that level to buy the dip.

✦ Key Insight
Support and resistance are zones, not exact prices. The more times a level has been tested, the more significant it becomes.
Lesson 3 of 5
The Flip — Role Reversal

One of the most powerful concepts in technical analysis: when a resistance level breaks, it becomes support. And vice versa.

This happens because the same price level attracts the same participants — but their roles switch after the break.

Lesson 4 of 5
How to Draw Them
1
Use the body of candles, not just the wicks. Wicks show extremes but bodies show where price spent most time.
2
Look for at least 2 touches. One touch is a coincidence. Two or more touches make a level significant.
3
Draw zones, not lines. A level is rarely a single exact price — draw a horizontal zone around the area.
4
Higher timeframes win. A level on the weekly chart is far more significant than one on the 15-minute chart.
5
Keep it clean. If your chart has 20 lines, you've drawn too many. Focus on the 2–3 most obvious ones.
⚠️ Most common mistake: Drawing too many levels until the chart becomes noise. Fewer, stronger levels are always better.
Lesson 5 of 5
How to Trade Them
Strategy 1 — Bounce Trade

Wait for price to reach a known support or resistance level. Look for a reaction candle (hammer, engulfing, doji). Enter in the direction of the bounce with your stop just beyond the level.

Strategy 2 — Breakout & Retest

Wait for price to break cleanly through a level on strong volume. Then wait for the retest of that broken level. Enter when price confirms the flip (resistance becomes support or vice versa).

✦ Final Truth
Support and resistance do not work every time. They increase probability. Combine them with candle patterns and volume for the highest-quality setups.
✅
You've completed the guide.

Now practice on real charts. Open any timeframe and draw only the 2–3 most obvious levels. Do this daily for 2 weeks and it becomes second nature.
